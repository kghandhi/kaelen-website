About Me

Kaelen Ghandhi is an improviser, saxophonist, composer, and educator based in New York with a Jazz Performance degree from The New School.

He derives inspiration from both modern jazz and improvised music. He develops this command by studying with artists from Tony Malaby, Joe Morris, and Andrew Cyrille to Seamus Blake, Joel Frahm, and Chris Cheek. Kaelen is forward thinking and highly creative about the music he makes, but can also be deeply swinging when he wants to be.

Kaelenâ€™s main instrument is tenor saxophone, but having trained on many instruments from a young age Kaelen is also proficient in flute and clarinet as well as alto, soprano and baritone saxophones. His main projects right now include work with Aaron Rubinstein and Kevin Murray in the improvising trio Ghandhi/Murray/Rubinstein. Additionally Kaelen has performed with William Parker, Michael Larocca and Jonathan Milberger among others.
